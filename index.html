INTEGRATED PATTERN READOUT
You eat 560–700 kcal below TDEE every day
Garmin puts your 1-year TDEE at 2,341 kcal/day (1,631 resting + 710 active). Your logged intake averages 1,760–1,850 kcal. That's a structural deficit of 500–580 kcal/day — sustained across every phase. Not a cutting phase, just your baseline.
Active calories track training type, not total volume
Jan–Mar: training volume dropped hard but Garmin active kcal barely moved (~680–720/day). High-HR cardio (BodyCombat) burns more per minute than cycling — so May's high session count didn't match the active kcal of a single 50-min Paris cardio. Intensity, not duration, drives that number.
The Jan–Mar dip: diet held, training dropped — and that's the whole story
Intake was flat at ~1,778–1,852 kcal through the dip. Training volume fell ~30%. But because active kcal only dipped ~60 kcal/day (high-HR cardio is efficient), total burn barely changed. Whatever body comp shift occurred in that window was smaller than it felt.
USA phase (May–Jun 13): volume explosion, intensity drop
Cycling sessions 3–5× per day added huge minute counts but Garmin active kcal for those weeks (May avg ~680/day) sat below Paris weeks with single high-HR cardio sessions. The mix also cut running to zero for 4 weeks — specificity cost that you're now paying back.
Carb drop mirrors intensity drop — and that's the risk
Carbs peaked in January (your highest-intensity month) then drifted down as training eased. Now that intensity is rebuilding (running back, high-HR cardio daily), carbs are still running ~15g/day below Jan levels. On heavy lower-body days that gap is where output suffers first.
Protein is the one variable you actually control well
Across 8 months and 3 distinct training phases, protein held 128–138g/day. That's the tightest of the three macros. The consistency is real — and it's the reason lean mass has been protected through the deficit. Keep it. The 125g floor needs to be non-negotiable even on low-cal days.
